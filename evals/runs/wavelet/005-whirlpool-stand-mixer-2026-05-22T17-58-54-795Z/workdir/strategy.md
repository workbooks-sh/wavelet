# Strategy — Whirlpool / KitchenAid Stand Mixer · 12s Meta Reels

## 1. Brand brief (adalign)

- **Whirlpool** — global appliance leader. Palette: black `#040404`, gold `#e3b40e`. Slogan: "Innovations that help you take on every load, meal and moment." Voice: caring, practical, family.
- **KitchenAid** (Whirlpool family brand) — iconic stand mixer since 1919. Palette: empire red `#c61430`, charcoal `#2c2c2c`. Slogan: "Bringing culinary inspiration to life since 1919." The Artisan stand mixer is the literal "iconic stand mixer" the user asked for.

The cleanest read of the brief: Whirlpool is the parent endorsement; the Artisan is the hero object. Co-branded close.

## 2. Liquid Death's own ads — N/A

Wrong scenario template. For Whirlpool the relevant scan: their Reels are mostly product-on-counter demos with utility copy ("removes 99% of stains") and a smiling family at the end. Functional, low-emotion. KitchenAid's organic Reels lean recipe-vertical: hands kneading dough, slow pours, bowl pulls. Mixed bag — some studio, some kitchen-handheld.

## 3. Competitive scan — adjacent premium appliances

- **Smeg** — pastel retro fetish-object framing, no hands, jewelry-box lighting. Strong gap: Smeg owns "appliance as decor."
- **Vitamix** — UGC-leaning recipe shots, kitchen handheld, demonstration-heavy. Owns "the appliance works hard."
- **Breville** — espresso-machine "first-pour" macro shots, slow shutter, café register. Owns "barista-grade at home."
- **Le Creuset** — heritage palette, single-pot hero, low-key warm light. Owns "the object you pass down."

Saturated patterns to avoid: pastel retro fetishization (Smeg), busy demonstration (Vitamix), espresso macro (Breville).

## 4. Synthesis

- **Strategic positioning for THIS spot** — The stand mixer as the centerpiece of a calm, weekend ritual. Not a feature ad, not a recipe demo — an atmosphere ad.
- **Audience state insight** — Sunday-night Reels scroller, thumb-paused on something quiet after a wall of fast-cut hooks. The premium frame is also the pattern-interrupt.
- **Three directions explored, one chosen + why**
  1. *Time-lapse: dough rising* — too literal, too long-haul for 12s.
  2. *Hands + ingredients montage, fast-cut to beat* — risks reading as Tasty, undercuts premium.
  3. *Hero studio object, locked register, single warm light, one match cut to the result* — CHOSEN. Restraint reads premium and gives the wordmark room to land.
- **Visual register choice** — Studio product-on-pedestal. Single warm key (kelvin ~3200), seamless cream backdrop, deep shadow side, slow push-in. Reads heritage / luxury — matches the "passed-down kitchen object" register the Artisan actually occupies in real homes.
- **What this AVOIDS** — No pastel-retro Smeg framing, no fast-cut Tasty montage, no busy demonstration with hands working the dial. Closed, calm, atmospheric.
