# Strategy — Whirlpool / KitchenAid Artisan stand mixer

## Cinematography lock

50 mm full-frame, shallow depth of field, warm morning window light from camera-left at 3200K, deep cream-and-walnut palette with the iconic Empire Red accent, gentle film grain, A24 domestic-warm color grade, locked 9:16 portrait

## CTA mode

lifestyle — reason: KitchenAid's owned spots favor mood ("For the Way It's Made") over direct-response. Tagline plate at close, no button, no URL.

## Brand logo handling

Do NOT render KitchenAid logos in generated frames. The iconic silhouette + Empire Red colorway is the identity carrier. Wordmark appears only as a final HTML overlay in the brand's typographic register (a clean modern serif, not the actual logo file — this is an editorial spec, not a delivered ad).

## Shot plan (12s, 4 shots × 3s)

1. **Hero open** — wide on a perfectly-styled kitchen counter at dawn, the mixer is the focal subject in Empire Red, soft light wraps it. Static / slow push.
2. **Macro action** — extreme close-up of the dough hook turning, batter folding over itself. Slow speed, no sound of motor needed (cello carries).
3. **Hands moment** — a baker's hands cracking an egg into the bowl while the planetary action continues. Human warmth.
4. **Pull-back close** — slow pull back from the finished mixer, golden hour light, dust motes in the air, the kitchen reads as "lived in, loved."

Hero tier (full Veo 3.1) for shots 1 and 4. Fast tier for shots 2 and 3.

## Editorial overlay variation rule

- Scene 1: editorial silence (vignette only, let the shot breathe)
- Scene 2: tiny mono corner tag ("EST. 1919 · ARTISAN")
- Scene 3: zero text
- Scene 4: lifestyle close — serif tagline "At the heart of home" with KitchenAid wordmark, knockout-style via mix-blend-mode: difference for premium register
