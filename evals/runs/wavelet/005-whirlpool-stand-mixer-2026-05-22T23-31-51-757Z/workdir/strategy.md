# Strategy — Whirlpool stand mixer spot

## Concept

A 12-second love letter to a single object on a counter. The mixer is
treated as a piece of architectural sculpture, not an appliance. Warm
morning light, hand-on-product intimacy, then a hero pull-back. Last
2s is a direct-response CTA card with the wordmark and a button.

## Cinematography lock

50 mm full-frame, medium-close framing, warm morning window light
camera-left at 3200K, deep cream-and-brown palette, gentle film grain,
A24 domestic-warm grade, locked 9:16 portrait

Every shot prompt ends with the above clause, character-for-character.

## CTA mode

direct_response — reason: this is a hard-good purchase, the spot ends
on a Shop button + wordmark over a final held product beauty shot.

## Shot plan (7 cuts, 12s total)

- `0.0-1.5 s` — close: hand sets a stainless bowl onto the mixer base, click of the lock
- `1.5-3.0 s` — macro: the tilt-head lowering, beater nearing the bowl
- `3.0-4.5 s` — top-down: butter and sugar folding under the paddle, ribbon forming
- `4.5-6.5 s` — slow push-in on the cast-metal enamel hip of the mixer, light raking across the curve (REST)
- `6.5-8.0 s` — flour swirling down the side of the bowl, mid-mix
- `8.0-10.0 s` — wide hero: the pearl-white mixer alone on a warm walnut counter, soft morning light
- `10.0-12.0 s` — CTA: wordmark + "Shop the iconic mixer" button over a held still of the mixer

## Variation across cuts

- Scene 1-6: zero overlay text. Footage speaks.
- Scene 7 (CTA): brand wordmark in Bodoni-style serif, button in cream
  on a deep terracotta fill (Whirlpool brand-adjacent warmth, not
  identity-claiming red).

## Pacing

Hard cuts throughout. No crossfades except a deliberate 0.4s dip-to-
black between the wide hero (scene 6) and the CTA (scene 7) to mark
the editorial gear-change from atmosphere to call.
