# Strategy — KitchenAid Artisan stand mixer (Whirlpool Corp.)

## Concept

Three slow, warm, observational beats. No talking head. No voiceover.
The mixer does the acting. We close on the iconic silhouette and a
single line of editorial copy.

## Cinematography lock

50 mm full-frame, medium framing with macro inserts, warm morning
window light camera-left at 3200K, deep brown-cream-brass palette,
gentle film grain, A24 domestic-warm grade, locked 9:16 portrait

Every shot prompt ends with the comma-prefixed clause above,
character-for-character.

## Palette

- Cream #f5efe6 (highlights, type)
- Burnt sienna #b3582a (mixer body, brand red leaning toward terracotta)
- Espresso #3a2418 (deep shadow, type on cream)
- Brushed brass #b4945c (fixtures, accent)
- Window white #fffaf2 (key light)

## Typography

Bodoni Moda (display, italic for emotional line) + Inter (kicker,
micro caps). One typeface change per scene. No reused lockup.

## Shot list (3 shots × 4s = 12s)

1. **EXT. KITCHEN COUNTER — MORNING** — wide-ish, 9:16: a creamy
   KitchenAid Artisan stand mixer on a butcher-block counter, soft
   window light from camera-left. A hand sets down a brown egg next
   to it. Very still composition; the mixer doesn't move.

2. **MACRO INSERT — DOUGH HOOK TURNING** — extreme close on the
   spiral dough hook folding a pale buttery dough over itself,
   slow rotation, soft window light glinting on the burnished metal.

3. **PULL-BACK / HERO SILHOUETTE** — slow dolly back from the mixer,
   tilt-head closed, soft morning haze. A woman's hand briefly
   enters frame to lift the head. The iconic silhouette holds the
   center. Caption space lives in upper-third.

## CTA mode

direct_response — reason: KitchenAid's published Meta ads consistently
end with brand wordmark + tagline + shop button. Last 1.5s is a
dedicated CTA card layered over scene 3's hold frame.

## Motion lock

Editorial pacing. Average shot length ~4s. No whip pans, no flashy
transitions. Crossfade between 1↔2 (0.4s), straight cut into 3.
Captions enter on extended `var(--ease-out-quint)` curves, not plain
ease.
