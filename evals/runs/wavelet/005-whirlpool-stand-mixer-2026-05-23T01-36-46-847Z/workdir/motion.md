# Whirlpool — Stand Mixer (12s, 9:16, Meta Reels)

## Premise
A 12-second luxury kitchen brand spot for the Whirlpool stand mixer. Sells craft, ritual, and inheritance — not features.

## Palette
- **Hero amber** `#C8862E` — late-afternoon kitchen light, brass trim
- **Whirlpool red** `#E0241B` — brand accent, used twice and never larger than the type
- **Marble white** `#F4F0E8` — warm-tone off-white, kitchen surfaces
- **Charcoal** `#1A1614` — type, deep shadow
- Black bars / ink stays out. No pure white, no pure black.

## Motion language
- Slow. Every move under 0.6 px/frame. The mixer rotates. The whisk turns. The light shifts.
- Two micro pushes in 12s — one on the hero, one on the close. Never a hard cut without an exit tween.
- Type rises 24px on EaseOutExpo over 0.7s, fades in to 100%. Exits with a 0.35s fade.

## Type system
- Display: tight tracked serif (Cormorant Garamond fallback to Georgia), weight 500.
- Eyebrow: small caps sans, 1.6em letter-spacing, weight 600.
- Logo lockup: Whirlpool wordmark in red on warm white, bottom-anchored, 80px from the bottom safe-zone.

## Pacing
- 0.0–4.0s — Shot A — silhouetted mixer on marble, side light, slow push-in. Eyebrow: "FOR THE ONES WHO STILL MAKE IT BY HAND"
- 4.0–8.0s — Shot B — macro of whisk turning cream into stiff peaks, slow ribbons. Headline rises: "Built to be passed down."
- 8.0–12.0s — Shot C — wide kitchen, mixer at rest, soft natural light, no people. Logo: "Whirlpool" + tag "Made for makers, since 1911."

## Identity guardrails
- No human faces (avoids identity-drift, keeps subject the mixer).
- Mixer never spins fast — beats on low.
- No on-screen prices, no CTAs ("Shop Now" feels cheap at this tier).
- The product is the hero. Type is in service of it.
