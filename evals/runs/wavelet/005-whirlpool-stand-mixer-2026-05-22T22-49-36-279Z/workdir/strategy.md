# Strategy — Whirlpool stand mixer spot

## Cinematography lock

50mm full-frame, medium-close framing, warm morning window light from camera-left at 3200K, deep brown-and-cream palette with a single saturated red accent on the mixer body, gentle film grain, A24 domestic-warm grade, locked 9:16 portrait

## CTA mode

Lifestyle — reason: KitchenAid (the iconic Whirlpool-owned stand mixer brand) leans hard on aspirational "for the love of food" framing in their long-form ads, not direct-response CTAs. Last beat holds atmospheric on the mixer with a fading "WHIRLPOOL" wordmark + "At the heart of home." tagline, no button, no URL.

## Spot structure (12s, three 4s beats)

1. **0.0-4.0** — Wide hero. Cream-and-oak kitchen at morning light. The iconic stand mixer (red enamel cast body, polished bowl) sits center-frame on a butcher block. Slow push-in. No type.
2. **4.0-8.0** — Macro. The dough hook turns, folding shaggy dough into a smooth elastic mass. Steam wisps. Type: a quiet two-line moment in editorial Didone — *"Every Sunday. / Every birthday."*
3. **8.0-12.0** — Pull-back. Two hands lift the bowl free of the mixer; the mixer stays, gleaming, ready for the next bake. Closing wordmark "WHIRLPOOL" fades up small with the tagline below.

## Type palette (no two scenes share lockup)

- Scene 1 — no type (editorial silence rule)
- Scene 2 — Bodoni Moda italic, two short lines, lower-left, drift-up animation on `ease-out-quint`
- Scene 3 — Bodoni Moda regular wordmark center-bottom, very small caps with wide tracking on a luxury-whisper entry

## Motion language

Slow. Every entry on extended easings (quint / expo / circ) — nothing on plain `ease`. Two scenes do crossfades (0.5s); the title-card-free scene 1 to scene 2 cut is a hard cut to keep the cadence honest.
