# Strategy — Liquid Death, 12s Meta Reels/Stories (9:16)

## 1.1 Brand brief (from `adalign brief liquiddeath.com`)

- Wordmark + can art are the signature visual — gothic blackletter LIQUID DEATH lockup, skull motif, "Mountain Water" descriptor in tall slab caps.
- Palette: `#141414` (matte black), `#170d9b` (deep punk blue), `#8486ba` (washed lavender). Effectively black-and-near-black with a single chromatic deviation.
- Typography signal: Acumin Pro for body; the brand wordmark is custom gothic.
- Voice: irreverent mock-horror; death-metal jokes about wholesome beverages. Tagline anchors: *Murder Your Thirst*, *Death to Plastic*.
- Audience: Gen-Z / younger Millennials who buy soft drinks as identity merch. 7.5M IG followers, 7.2M TikTok. Their content does numbers because it does NOT look like beverage advertising.
- Distribution: heavy Meta + TikTok presence; running 30+ active Meta ads right now (paginated).

## 1.2 Liquid Death's own current Meta ads

10 ads pulled. Patterns:

1. **Sweepstakes flood (6 of 10):** *"Win A Killer House — Liquid Death pouring out of every faucet"* — Sodahouse promo with Taylor Morrison Homes. Both static carousel and creator-UGC video versions. CTA: Sign Up / See Details.
2. **Merch tie-ins (3 of 10):** *Eternal Death Zip Hoodie*, *Putt to Death Golf Polo*. Body copy like *"Cover your entire home + body in Liquid Death merch to show your thirst who's boss."* Static lifestyle product shots.
3. **Creator stunt UGC (1 of 10):** *"Testing out @liquiddeath Energy inside of a tornado simulator. Get some now at your local Wal-Mart 🔥#liquiddeathpartner #ad"* — phone footage, creator on-camera, bright daylight.
4. Across the board: title copy leans wordplay (*Par for the Hearse*, *Putt to Death*, *Eternal Death*), CTA chiclets are Sign Up / Shop Now / See Details, visual register **varies wildly between sweepstakes graphic, lifestyle product, and creator UGC**.
5. **No restrained / cinematic / quiet treatments visible.** The brand's paid social is comedy and graphic-layout heavy; the brand's *organic mythology* (death metal, ritual, gothic) shows up on can art but not in the running ads.

## 1.3 Competitive + industry scan

**Celsius (energy):** 6 ads. Heavy flavor-launch cadence — *CELSIUS ELECTRIC VIBE™*, *Limited Edition*, *Available at Target*. Bodies hammer *"tropical fruit"*, *"vibrant flavor"*, *"refreshing burst"*. Pattern: bright gradient backgrounds, single can hero floating, name of flavor as the only graphic. CTAs: Learn more / Shop now. They're all variations on one ad.

**Olipop (better-for-you soda):** 6 ads. Two anchors — health-benefit cards (*"90% of Americans don't get enough fiber"*) and recipe UGC (*"@bevsbybeverly Sherbet Champagne Floats"*). Pastel palette, deliberate "feed-native creator" feel, lots of static product against light backgrounds.

**Athletic Brewing (NA beer):** 6 ads. The full retention playbook — *Limited Time Welcome Offer 🎁 / 25% off / Free shipping / Try Athletic / All the flavor None of the hangover*. Mostly product packshots + discount banner.

**Patterns saturated in the category right now:**
- Floating can hero over vibrant gradient background (Celsius, Bang, most energy)
- Sweepstakes / coupon / "Win a [thing]" graphic (LD itself, Athletic)
- Creator-eats / creator-drinks UGC, bright daylight (LD energy tornado stunt, Olipop recipe carousels)
- Pastel-and-stock-shadow product layout (Olipop)
- Verbal pyrotechnics ("ELECTRIC VIBE", "Fruity Explosions", "Murder Your Thirst")

**The gap:** nobody in the cluster — including Liquid Death itself — is doing **brutal quiet**. A 12-second ad that treats canned water with the visual gravity of a horror short. LD's *mythology* is gothic but its *paid creative* is loud comedy + sweepstakes. We can occupy the brand's own unclaimed register.

## 1.4 Synthesis

**Strategic positioning for THIS spot:** A 12-second iPhone-shot ritual ad — Liquid Death played dead-straight as a horror talisman, no jokes, no gradient, no sweepstakes graphic. It looks like a fan's video, not a brand's ad — which is exactly how LD's organic universe already feels and exactly what its paid creative currently is *not* doing.

**Audience state insight:** Late-night Reels scroller, ~11pm couch-scroll, sound on, thumb idle. They're being served Celsius gradient cans and Olipop fiber graphics and LD sweepstake cards — pattern-matched as ads in 0.4s and flicked past. A 1.5s opener that looks like UGC, not an ad, beats the algorithmic muscle-memory that triggers a swipe.

**Three creative directions considered, one chosen + why:**
1. *"The Cult of Liquid Death"* — chosen. Black-clothed hands raise a Mountain Water can like a chalice in candlelight, on a friend's kitchen counter. iPhone footage. No music until beat 6, then a single low drone, then the wordmark hit + CTA. *Why:* it owns the unclaimed serious-gothic register the can art already promises, while reading as fan-cam UGC (the format Meta's algorithm rewards on this audience).
2. *"Murder Your Thirst — courtroom"* — a wordless mock-trial where the can "convicts" tap-water. Too writerly for 12 seconds.
3. *"Sparkling Water Heavy Metal Concert"* — over-saturated, indistinguishable from LD's existing comedy stunts, fights for attention with their own running ads.

**Visual register choice — Organic iPhone footage, locked.** Handheld but tight (not flailing), available candlelight at 4500K with a single window-light kicker, deep DoF (iPhone wide lens at f/1.78 native, no fake bokeh), iPhone HDR color science, mild rolling-shutter wobble preserved. **Same lens character, same lighting setup, same grade on every shot.** Repeated cinematography vocabulary in every Veo prompt: *handheld iPhone, 4500K candlelight, single window kicker, deep DoF, mild rolling shutter, HDR color, no color grade beyond stock iPhone*.

**What this AVOIDS:**
- The **"slow-mo can hero floating against vibrant gradient background"** pattern that Celsius, Bang, Alani, and most energy brands lean on. No gradient backgrounds, no float, no studio key light, no rotating product turntable. If a shot ever looks like a Celsius packshot, it's wrong.
- Liquid Death's own current **"sweepstakes / graphic-card / Win-A-House"** pattern. No flat 2-column graphic layout, no sweepstakes overlay, no Sign-Up CTA chiclet on a flat colored panel.
- The bright daylight creator-UGC stunt (LD tornado-simulator). Ours is candlelight, late, indoors, quiet.
