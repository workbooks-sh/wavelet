# Strategy — Liquid Death 12s Meta Reels / Stories

## 1.1 Brand brief (from adalign brief liquiddeath.com)

- **Brand**: Liquid Death — mountain water + sparkling water + iced tea + better-for-you energy, in infinitely recyclable aluminum cans.
- **Positioning**: comedy + horror + heavy-metal aesthetic against a wellness category that defaults to pastel. "Making health and sustainability fun."
- **Slogan**: "Murder Your Thirst."
- **Palette (brand site)**: `#141414` (near-black) / `#170d9b` (deep electric indigo) / `#8486ba` (cool ash blue).
- **Type**: Acumin Pro (heavy weights for headings). Live ads use a chiseled metal-band wordmark (the can-printed mark).
- **Audience signals (per social profiles)**: skews young, ironic, heavy-metal/death-metal-adjacent, environmentally aware. Their handles: @liquiddeath across IG/TikTok/X/FB/YT.

## 1.2 Liquid Death's own current Meta ads (5 visible)

Pattern across the 5 active ads:
- **2× sweepstakes "Win A House Filled With Liquid Death"** — long-copy SIGN_UP card collations, co-branded with Taylor Morrison.
- **1× co-branded sweepstakes follow-up** (Taylor Morrison page).
- **1× merch — "Par for the Hearse" golf polo**, SHOP_NOW CTA, horror–golf wordplay.
- **All carry the same caption**: `liquiddeath.com`, sign-up / shop-now CTAs, copy-heavy cards.

**What's missing from their current paid mix**: there is no straight 12-second brand-mood spot. Everything live right now is **transactional** — a stunt, a sweepstakes, a merch SKU. The brand voice (theatrical horror) is implicit in the copy but not the visual register.

## 1.3 Competitive scan

- **Celsius**: clean studio product cards on saturated hero color, vivid CTAs ("ELECTRIC VIBE™", "Available at Target"). High-key lighting, locked-off product hero, sticker-y graphic overlays. **Saturated pattern**: slow-mo product pour + neon gradient + capital-letter flavor name.
- **Olipop**: stats-led headlines ("90% of Americans don't get enough fiber"), pastel palette, daylight kitchen-counter shots. **Saturated pattern**: education + warm pastel + SHOP_NOW.
- **Athletic Brewing**: lifestyle hangout shots, "all the flavor, none of the hangover" copy at retailer. **Saturated pattern**: friends-around-a-cooler / golden hour patio.
- **Poppi**: retailer placements ("at Circle K"), bright candy palette. **Saturated pattern**: candy gradients + flavor-stack hero.

**The gap nobody is filling** in feed right now: a single-product, **mood-piece** spot with locked cinematography that respects the viewer enough to not over-CTA. Everyone in the category is either screaming a SKU drop, screaming a stat, or screaming a sweepstakes.

## 1.4 Synthesis

- **Strategic positioning for THIS spot** — A 12-second cursed-VHS pseudo-commercial that treats Liquid Death Mountain Water like a relic recovered from a haunted basement; ends on the wordmark as a typographic stamp and "MURDER YOUR THIRST" as a CTA punch. It does what Liquid Death's *brand* does but their *ads* currently don't: lead with the horror register first, sell second.
- **Audience state insight** — Meta-feed scrolling, late-evening, sound-off by default, half-attention. The hook has to read in 1.5 seconds with no audio. VHS jitter + a single ominous title card hits the "wait what is this" reflex faster than a clean product shot. (Pattern interrupt > product reveal at frame 1.)
- **Three creative directions explored, one chosen**:
  - *(A)* Studio product pedestal with slow rotate — REJECTED, that's the Celsius lane.
  - *(B)* Found-footage VHS horror short ending on can + wordmark — **CHOSEN**. Hooks on mute, fits the brand's horror DNA, deliberately differs from Liquid Death's current sweepstakes-led paid mix.
  - *(C)* UGC iPhone "kid finds can in glacier" gag — REJECTED, comedy register collides with the heavy-metal album-cover dignity the can deserves.
- **Visual register choice** — **Mock-horror VHS** (chromatic aberration, scanlines, mild frame jitter, low-fi color, deep shadow, single tungsten key light, locked-off lens). Repeated verbatim in every Veo prompt so the register holds across cuts.
- **What this AVOIDS**:
  - The Celsius/Bang "neon-gradient slow-mo product pour" — no clean white pedestal, no high-key key light, no slow-rotate.
  - Olipop's pastel daylight kitchen aesthetic — no warm sunlit countertops.
  - Athletic Brewing's lifestyle-patio hangout register — no friends, no golden hour, no group laughter.
  - Liquid Death's own current ad pattern of long-copy sweepstakes cards — this spot is mood-first, almost no copy, single CTA.
